 ABOUT THE METROMARKET     THE ST. LOUIS REALITY     OUR MISSION 

A food desert is a “low-income census tract where a substantial number or share of residents has low access to a supermarket or grocery store.”- USDA

Access to healthy, affordable food is not guaranteed for the roughly 52,000 adults and 14,000 children who live in any one of the 15 St. Louis City food deserts. The USDA defines a food desert as “a low-income census tract where a substantial number or share of residents has low access to a supermarket or grocery store.” While this disparity is present throughout the US, St. Louis’ population living in food deserts is 233% higher than the national average.

St. Louis food deserts realize overall poverty rates, and, most alarming, child poverty rates above the national average, 157% and 148% respectively. Food deserts are disproportionately poor, majority-minority, and for the families living in them, the health impact is marked, manifesting in every form of malnutrition along the spectrum from starvation to morbid obesity.

We will modify a city transit bus to create a grocery store on wheels and will sell fresh, healthy, locally sourced fruits, vegetables, meat, dairy, and staple goods directly in the communities that need them the most. We will incentivize healthy eating by keeping our food affordable and to accomplish this, we will utilize a sliding scale membership to subsidize operations in food deserts by concurrently selling our high-quality foods at local corporate campuses at competitive market prices. Through a strategic partnership with SLU’s Nutrition and Dietetics Department we will also offer free, weekly lessons on healthy eating and cooking demonstrations at the curbside.
